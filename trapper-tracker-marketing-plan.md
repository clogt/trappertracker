## 5. Paid Ads: Facebook/Instagram Campaign Strategy

**Goal:** Hyper-local, low-cost campaigns to drive initial user adoption.

---

### Ad Copy Variations (Short & Punchy)

1.  **Safety-First:** "Pet trappers are active in [Your City]. See where they've been reported and keep your dog or cat safe. Use our free, real-time community map. #PetSafety"
2.  **Lost/Found Focus:** "Lost pet in [Your City]? Found a wandering dog? Our free community map helps reunite lost pets with their families. See the map now. #LostDog #[YourCity]"
3.  **Community Angle:** "Protect the pets of [Your City]. Join your neighbors on TrapperTracker, the free community-powered map for reporting pet dangers and finding lost animals. #Community"
4.  **Direct Question:** "Do you know where the pet danger zones are in your neighborhood? See the community-reported map for free. Keep your pets safe. #TrapperTracker"

### Headlines (Under 40 Characters)

- [Your City] Pet Safety Map
- Protect Your Pet
- Free Trapper Alert Map
- Lost Pet? Check Here
- Community Pet Watch

### Call-to-Action (CTA) Buttons

- **Learn More** (Most neutral, good for initial awareness)
- **Use App** (Direct and clear, as it's a web app)
- **Sign Up** (Only if you have a user registration feature)

### Ad Targeting Recommendations

- **Location:** ZIP codes within [Your City] + 5-10 mile radius. Start small and local.
- **Age:** 25-65+
- **Detailed Targeting (Interests & Behaviors):**
    - **Interests:** Dog park, Pet adoption, Petfinder, Chewy, Petco, PetSmart, *and* must also match *local community groups*.
    - **Behaviors:** Pet owners (Facebook category).
    - **Layering:** Target people who like "Dog park" AND are "Pet owners".
- **Lookalike Audiences:** Once you have ~100 users, you can create a Lookalike Audience to find people similar to your existing users. This is very powerful.

### Budget & A/B Testing

- **Budget:** Start small ($5-$10 per day). Focus on a single, well-defined geographic area.
- **A/B Testing:**
    - **Test Creative:** Run the same ad copy with two different images (e.g., a map screenshot vs. a photo of a dog on a leash).
    - **Test Copy:** Run the same image with two different ad copy variations (e.g., Safety-First vs. Lost/Found Focus).
    - Let ads run for 3-5 days before deciding on a winner.

### Ad Creative Suggestions

- **Image 1:** A clean, compelling screenshot of the TrapperTracker map with several different icons visible in a familiar area.
- **Image 2:** A high-quality, emotionally resonant photo of a person walking their dog, with a subtle graphic overlay saying "Keep Them Safe."
- **Video (Advanced):** A 15-second screen recording showing how easy it is to view the map and make a report on a phone.

## 8. Content Strategy: First Month Calendar

**Goal:** Build momentum and create a habit of checking/using TrapperTracker.

---

### Week 1: Launch & Introduction
- **Mon:** Announce TrapperTracker in 3-5 key local Facebook groups (use Template #3).
- **Wed:** Post on Nextdoor (use Template #1).
- **Fri:** Share a "success story" if one exists, or a "how-to" post showing how to make a report.
- **Throughout:** Drop helpful comments on relevant "lost pet" or "sighting" posts (Templates #4 & #5).

### Week 2: Build Credibility & Partnerships
- **Mon:** Send emails to 10-15 local vets and pet stores (Email Template #1).
- **Wed:** Post a "Weekly Safety Tip" related to pet safety (e.g., "Tip: Always check your yard for foreign objects before letting your dog out.").
- **Fri:** Share a screenshot of the current map (blurring sensitive locations if needed) and say "Thanks to you, our community safety map is growing! Here's a look at the reports from this week."

### Week 3: Expand Reach
- **Mon:** Send emails to 5-10 local shelters and rescues (Email Template #2).
- **Wed:** Launch a small, targeted Facebook Ad campaign ($5/day) in a specific ZIP code.
- **Fri:** Post a "Myth vs. Fact" about pet safety or local wildlife.

### Week 4: Drive Engagement
- **Mon:** Pitch your story to a local blogger or pet influencer (Pitch Template).
- **Wed:** Run a poll in a Facebook group: "What's your biggest pet safety concern? a) Traps, b) Traffic, c) Wildlife, d) Off-leash dogs."
- **Fri:** Post a "Thank You" to the community, sharing user numbers (e.g., "We now have over 200 local pet owners using the map!") and reinforcing the mission.

## 9. Viral Growth Strategies (That Don't Violate ToS)

1.  **The "Share to See" Mechanic (Soft Version):** When a user submits a report, the confirmation message can say: "Your report has been added! Help make this map even more powerful by sharing it with 3 pet-loving friends or in your favorite local Facebook group." This encourages sharing at the moment of highest engagement.

2.  **Embeddable Map Widget:** Create a simple HTML snippet that allows local shelters, rescues, or bloggers to embed a view of the TrapperTracker map on their own websites. This provides value to them (live, local content) and serves as a constant ad for you.

3.  **"Found Pet" Posters with QR Codes:** When a user generates a "Found Pet" report, give them the option to auto-generate a simple, printable "Found Pet" flyer that includes the pet's photo, details, and a QR code linking directly to the report on the TrapperTracker map. This takes your brand offline and into neighborhoods.

4.  **Community "Safety Zone" Certificates:** A fun, digital "certificate" that a neighborhood can earn once they have a certain number of active users or have gone a certain period without a negative incident. It fosters friendly competition between neighborhoods to sign up.

5.  **Partner with Micro-Influencers:** Don't just go for the big pet pages. Find the person who runs the local "Pug Owners of [Your City]" group of 200 people. A recommendation from a small, tight-knit community leader is often more powerful than a post from a large, impersonal page.
